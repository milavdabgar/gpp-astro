## NBA-NATIONAL BOARD OF ACCREDITATIONS

NBA  acronym stands  for 'National  Board  of  Accreditations' -  The  NBA  is  already  conducting accreditation of technical programs being run by the institutions. The AICTE Regulation recognizes NBA as an "assessment and accreditation agency" for the purpose of undertaking accreditation. The applying Engineering  institutions  have  to  establish  systematic  procedure  for  Teaching  and  learning  activities, student  support  &amp;  infrastructure  that  lead  to  NBA  and  it  is  playing  a  vital  role  for  enhancing  constant quality improvement in higher learning institutes.

NBA  accreditation  recognizes  the  students'  contributions,  innovations  and  achievements  towards  their technical  learning  happening  in  the  institutes.  The  accreditation  of  NBA  helps  the  higher  educational institute to know its strengths, weaknesses and opportunities and challenges which gives a clear road map to  improve  the  quality  in  Technical  Education  System.  Accreditation  is  important  because  it:  Helps  to recognizewhether  an  institution  meets  or  having  minimum  standards  of  quality.  Also  helps  the  students determine acceptable institutions for enrollment. Employers often require proper evidence that applicants have  received  a  degree  from  an  accredited  school  or  program.An  Expert  Team  constituted  by  NBA (National  Board  of  Accreditation)  New  Delhi  conducted  online/on-site  evaluation  of  the  programs from 24th to 26th September, 2021.

## CLIMATE CHANGE

The  changes  in  the  average  weather  conditions  in  the  given  region  or  area  over  the  time  is  Climate Change. This includes changes in temperature, wind patterns and precipitation. The change is referred in a global sense and concerns the earth as a whole.

Climate Change in Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change usage refers to a change in the state of the climate that can be identified (e.g. using statistical tests) by changes in the mean and/or the variability of  its  properties  and  that  persists  for  an  extended  period,  typically  decades  or  longer.  It  refers  to  any change in climate over time, whether due to natural variability or as a result of human activity.

## OBSERVED CHANGES

According to Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change, warming of the climate system is unequivocal, as is now evident from observations of increase in global average air and ocean temperatures, widespread melting of snow and ice and rising global average sea level.

-  Global temperature increase especially at higher northern latitudes.
-  Decline of mountain glaciers and snow cover in both hemispheres.
-  Ocean acidification.
-  Global average sea level rise at an average rate of 1.8 mm per year during 1961 to 2003 and 3.1 mm per year during 1993 to 2003.
-  Increase in intense tropical cyclone activity in the North Atlantic.
-  Changes in global precipitation pattern.
-  More intense and longer droughts over wider areas in the tropics and sub-tropics.
-  Increased heat-related mortality and infectious diseases.

## SAVE SOIL

Save  Soil  is  a  global  movement  launched  by  Isha  Foundation  (Sadhguru),  to  address  the  soil  crisis  by bringing together people from around the world to stand up for Soil Health, and supporting leaders of all nations to institute national policies and actions toward increasing the organic content in cultivable Soil. The lecture has been given by Dr. SachinMalve, Scientist (agronomy) Dantiwada Agriculture University. Dr.Sachin has explained the need of this movement and awaked with some interesting facts about the soil. Some of as below,

-  A study on nutrients in food concluded that we would have to eat 8 oranges to get the same amount of Vitamin A as our grandparents did with one orange because soil depletion has caused massive drops in nutrient levels in food. (Scientific American)
-  2 billion people suffer from nutritional deficiencies (WHO)
-  The average mineral content of calcium, magnesium, and iron in cabbage, lettuce, tomatoes, and spinach has dropped 80-90% between 1914 and 2018 in the US (NCBI)
-  The global economy will lose US $23 trillion by 2050 through land degradation (UNCCD)
-  Soil  degradation  causes  a  loss  of  $235  billion  to  $577  billion  in  crop  productivity  every year (IPBES)

The event become so interesting and students and faculties of the institute taken oath to plant one tree each year, and take care of it.

## CLEAN OCEAN MISSION

The Ocean covers more than 70% of the Earth's surface and contains more than 97% of the water on the planet.  The ocean's  health is  essential  for  all  life  on  Earth,  not  only  for  marine  wildlife.  Keeping our marine ecosystems clean from debris and pollution is  probably  one of  the  most  important  goals  we should work towards, and it is definitely one we should all care about. In the last decade, the discussion about  the  ocean  has  become  more  and  more  relevant.  Our  ocean  functions  as  the  Earth's  life  support system, and to ensure a sustainable future, we must protect and preserve its waters and ecosystems from all types of pollution.

There are many things we can do to clean our ocean and reduce the amount of pollution that enters it in the first place and to explain this 'Clean Ocean' program has been organized at our institute. Mr.Rakesh Prajapati,  Lecturer  Government  polytechnic  Palanpur,  has  explained  need  of  clean  ocean  mission  and explained following some ways to clean the ocean,

-  Reduce the Use of Plastic Items
-  Reduce Your Carbon Footprint
-  Support Non-profit Organizations and Research
-  Clean-Ups(simply do it on the weekend on your own, maybe involving your friends and family!)
-  Clean the Ocean's Garbage Patches
-  Eat Sustainable Fish (or No Fish at All!)

## GUJARAT 2022, NATIONAL GAMES OF INDIA

The 2022 National Games of India, also known as Gujarat 2022, was the 36th edition of the National games of India, and were held in Ahmedabad, Gandhinagar, Surat, Vadodara, Rajkot and Bhavnagar in the  state  of Gujarat between  27  September  and  10  October  2022.  The  official  logo  and  motto  of  the games was unveiled on 22 July 2022.

The logo depicts an Asiatic lion head make up of sporting pictograms and the Statue of Unity. The official motto of the games is "Celebrating unity through sports" .The sports program will include athletics, field hockey, football, volleyball, lawn tennis, table tennis, skateboarding, Netball and judo, and will also feature traditional sports such as kabaddi, kho-kho, mallakhamba, and yogasana.

An estimated 7,000 athletes from 28 states and eight union territories are expected to participate in 36 disciplines,  which  comprise most of  the traditional  Olympic sports.  The  National  Games  2022 will  be held across 6 cities in Gujarat, viz., Gandhinagar, Surat, Ahmedabad, Rajkot, Vadodara, and Bhavnagar. To spread awareness of this event,  Mr.  B  G  Parmar  City  Mamlatdar,  Palanpur  came  to  our  institute Government Polytechnic Palanpur On 09th September 2022. He has proudly explained the importance of this event at our country especially at Gujarat and also explained the importance of sports in our life .All the students and faculties has participated in this event enthusiastically.
